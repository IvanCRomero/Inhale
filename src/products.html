Hardware

Sensor MH-Z19C

The MH-Z19 sensor measures the concentration of Carbon Dioxide (CO2) in the air. It has a range of 0 to 5000ppm, temperature compensation and the measurement is independent of the oxygen level. It has two output types: UART and PWM.

Unlike MQ gas sensors that use the variation of a resistance depending on the gas (electrochemical), the MH-Z19 sensor uses a non-dispersive infrared (NDIR) sensor. Inside the sensor is a small chamber where the gas is subjected to infrared light, the CO2 gas absorbs a certain wavelength in the IR light beam according to the gas concentration and the sensor measures the difference within this wavelength, giving the CO2 concentration in parts per million (ppm).
